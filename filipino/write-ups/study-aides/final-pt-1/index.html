So if you Google 'nasaan' vs 'saan' you'll see a bunch of different things. Since Tita's the one's grading the test final, her rules are the ones presented below:

'Nasaan' is used to ask where something is at. Most often, this means you should use 'nasaan' before nouns and such.

'Saan' is used to ask where an activity is occurring at, where something is being done, or to inquire about the destination. Usually, you should use 'saan' before verbs.

Hopefully you can recognize verbs and adjectives, but if you can't, you can still just sort of finesse the answer by looking for mga's or adjectives with repeated syllables that are also linked. If either of those are after the space, check if nasaan would make sense.

A similar concept applies with verbs: if you can recognize that a verb has one of the verb affixes that we've worked with (um, mag, ma, mang, man, mam, in/hin, an/han, i).

The only thing to watch out for is that some adjectives with a repeated syllable might look like a conjugation of a ma- verb.
